One of the primary factors affecting productivity
and human health is stress, and effective intervention depends on
its precise identification. Traditional stress detection techniques
utilize costly and complex sensors, which are not feasible for
widespread deployment. In this study, an efficient approach to
detecting stress levels is proposed, based on open-access EEG
data and lightweight machine learning algorithms. EEG signal
preprocessing from the DEAP dataset, segmentation, and feature
extraction in time and frequency domains were performed. The
stress levels were labeled into three classes: low, medium, and
high. Three machine learning models, Support Vector Machine
(SVM), Random Forest, and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), were
trained and evaluated on the basis of accuracy, precision, recall,
F1-score, and confusion matrices. The results exhibited high
precision with Random Forest and KNN, demonstrating that
stress can be accurately detected without utilizing complex
sensors. The approach provides a viable solution for real-time
stress monitoring in future mobile and wearable devices.
